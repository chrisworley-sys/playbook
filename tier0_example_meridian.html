01 / Observations

What We Observed

Four areas where we noticed patterns worth thinking about — starting with the broader category context and then narrowing to your specific surfaces.

1

The Bay Area wealth advisory category has consolidated around three messaging archetypes.

Market
Observation

Across the dozen-plus Bay Area RIAs we looked at, messaging clusters around three patterns: holistic-comprehensive planning, fiduciary-fee-only positioning, and niche-specialty depth. The first two represent the established category default. The third is where the interesting movement is happening.

What this may indicate

When a category becomes saturated with similar messaging, niche-specialty positioning typically wins inbound. In your case, stock-comp planning for tech professionals is exactly that kind of niche — but it's worth understanding how prospects in your region actually search and discover firms in this space.

The opportunity

Firms that lead with specialty depth in their public surfaces tend to convert higher in your category. There's a real opening here to occupy the "stock-comp planning" position more clearly than any current competitor has.

2

Your stock-comp specialty is present but doesn't lead.

Digital Market
Observation

The phrase "stock-comp planning" appears on your services page, three scrolls down. Your homepage hero leads with "comprehensive financial planning" — language that matches the category default we saw in observation 1.

What this may indicate

The thing that most differentiates Meridian — the stock-comp specialty — may be undersold in the highest-traffic area of your site. We'd want to investigate whether this reflects a deliberate positioning choice (e.g., concern about narrowing the funnel) or simply a homepage structure that grew organically.

The opportunity

Homepages that lead with specialty depth tend to outperform generalist framing in saturated categories. Worth exploring whether a specialty-led restructure fits your current positioning roadmap.

3

AI search currently surfaces competitors for your niche queries.

SEO/AEO Market
Observation

When we asked ChatGPT and Perplexity for "advisors specializing in stock compensation in the Bay Area," two competitor firms appeared. Meridian did not. Querying directly for "Meridian Wealth Partners" returned general information but didn't highlight the stock-comp specialty.

What this may indicate

AI search models tend to source from indexed content that has accumulated over time — blog posts, third-party mentions, structured About content. If the specialty isn't documented in those sources, the AI doesn't know to mention it. Worth investigating which sources currently shape your AI search presence.

The opportunity

AI search visibility compounds over 12-18 months as content gets indexed. Firms that establish their specialty in this layer earlier tend to be the ones AI tools cite by default — a meaningful long-term tailwind.

4

Cadence varies meaningfully across your content surfaces.

Content Strategy Social SEO/AEO
Observation

Your blog hasn't published since March of last year. LinkedIn shows steady posting from your founder, but the firm's company page is quieter. There's no newsletter or recurring content surface we could find.

What this may indicate

In categories like wealth advisory, prospects often research across multiple surfaces before reaching out — and content cadence is one of the signals they read for momentum. The pattern here could reflect a deliberate channel deprioritization, or it could be an area where attention has simply slipped. Worth investigating which.

The opportunity

A consistent content cadence — even at 1-2 pieces per month — tends to be high-leverage for SEO and AI search visibility over time. For a specialty firm, this is also where you build the documented expertise that AI tools index.

02 / Pattern

The Pattern We're Seeing

The pieces of a strong specialty position are already here — but they're scattered across surfaces in a way that doesn't compound, and competitors are filling the gap in AI search where you could be leading.

  • Your stock-comp specialty exists in your offering, but doesn't lead in your most-trafficked surfaces.
  • AI search is currently citing competitors who have done the indexable groundwork — a window that's closing.
  • Content cadence inconsistency means the documented expertise that would shift AI visibility isn't accumulating.
03 / Implications

What This Suggests

Four areas where the pattern points toward meaningful opportunity. None of these are prescriptions — they're directions worth exploring further.

Specialty-led positioning is the highest-leverage opportunity.

Market Digital

The category is saturated with generalist messaging, and the firms breaking out in your space are doing so on specialty depth. Worth exploring how aggressively to lean into the stock-comp focus across your public surfaces — homepage, services, About, and outbound messaging.

AI search visibility compounds — earlier intervention pays disproportionately.

SEO/AEO

The way AI tools cite firms today is shaped by content indexed over the previous 12-18 months. Establishing your specialty in indexable sources now means AI tools will cite you by default a year from now — when that visibility becomes much harder to acquire.

A modest content cadence often unlocks outsized visibility gains.

Content Strategy SEO/AEO

For specialty firms, 1-2 pieces per month focused on the niche is often enough to shift AI search positioning meaningfully over 6-12 months. The bar isn't volume — it's specificity and consistency.

Channel consolidation tends to outperform channel expansion.

Social Content Strategy

Rather than adding new social surfaces, firms in your category often see better returns from tightening cadence on the channels already showing momentum. Your founder's LinkedIn presence is a real asset — worth investigating how to compound it.
